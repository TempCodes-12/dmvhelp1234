How to Get Your Driver's License in the US as an International Student

Your step-by-step guide to navigating the process in Missouri.

To apply for a Missouri driver's license, international students must first obtain a letter from the Social Security Administration (SSA) stating their ineligibility for a Social Security Number (SSN).

Steps to get the SSA letter:

  1. Visit your nearest SSA office. Use the SSA Office Locator to find one.
  2. Bring the following documents:
    • Passport
    • I-94 (Arrival/Departure Record)
    • I-20 (F-1 students) or DS-2019 (J-1 students)
  3. Request a letter of ineligibility for an SSN.

The written test covers Missouri traffic laws, road signs, and safe driving practices.

Study resources:

  • Download the Missouri Driver Guide.
  • Take online practice tests to familiarize yourself with the format and questions.

Tip: Focus on chapters about road signs and traffic laws, as these are heavily tested.

Before heading to the DMV, ensure you have the following documents ready:

  • Passport (valid and unexpired)
  • SSA letter of ineligibility
  • Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, lease agreement, or bank statement)
  • I-20 or DS-2019
  • I-94 (can be printed from CBP's website)

Tip: Bring both originals and photocopies of your documents.

After passing the written test, you will receive an instruction permit. This allows you to practice driving under the supervision of a licensed driver.

Key points:

  • The supervising driver must be at least 21 years old.
  • You must carry your permit at all times while driving.

The road test evaluates your ability to drive safely and follow traffic laws.

What to expect:

  • The examiner will assess your skills in areas like parking, lane changes, and obeying traffic signals.
  • You must provide a vehicle for the test. Ensure it is insured, registered, and in good condition.

Tips for success: Practice driving in various conditions (e.g., highways, residential areas) and review Missouri traffic laws and road signs.
